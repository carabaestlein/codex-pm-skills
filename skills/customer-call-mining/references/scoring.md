# Scoring

Use labels by default: `High`, `Medium`, `Low`.

Sort each section by impact first, then confidence.

## Impact

Impact reflects the likely product and business importance of an insight.

Consider:

- Strength of customer pain or opportunity
- ICP fit
- Revenue, expansion, deal, retention, or churn relevance
- Urgency
- Strategic fit with product priorities
- Product surface area or number of affected users

Use:

- **High**: Clear pain or opportunity from an ICP customer, tied to a deal/retention risk, urgent workflow, or strategic product area.
- **Medium**: Meaningful but less urgent, narrower segment, or moderate business relevance.
- **Low**: Interesting but weakly tied to ICP, revenue, urgency, or current strategy.

## Confidence

Confidence reflects evidence quality, not whether the idea sounds plausible.

Use:

- **High**: Direct customer statement, clear context, repeated or deeply discussed, little ambiguity.
- **Medium**: Clear but partial evidence, some interpretation needed, or repeated lightly.
- **Low**: Passing mention, sales interpretation without direct customer evidence, unclear speaker/context, or ambiguous transcript.

## Emphasis Versus Frequency

For one transcript:

- **Emphasis High**: The speaker returned to the topic, discussed it in depth, or framed it as central.
- **Emphasis Medium**: Mentioned more than once or discussed with moderate detail.
- **Emphasis Low**: Mentioned once, briefly, or in passing.

For multiple transcripts:

- Use `Frequency` as call count when possible, such as `3/8 calls`.
- Use `High`, `Medium`, or `Low` only when exact counts are not practical.

Never imply single-call emphasis equals broad market frequency.
