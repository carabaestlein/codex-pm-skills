# Audience And Tone

Use the config first. If no config is available, default to practical B2B SaaS copy for a knowledgeable but busy reader.

## Audience Defaults

- **Buyer**: Emphasize operational impact, risk reduction, visibility, governance, or team efficiency.
- **Admin/operator**: Emphasize workflow changes, setup, controls, permissions, and rollout details.
- **End user**: Emphasize what they can now do, where to find it, and what changed in the product.
- **Developer/technical user**: Include APIs, docs, prerequisites, migration notes, and limits.

## Tone

- Clear, specific, and low-hype.
- Confident without overclaiming.
- Short sentences.
- No filler such as "we are excited to announce" unless the config explicitly asks for it.
- Avoid vague claims like "seamless", "powerful", "next-generation", "game-changing", and "unlock".

## Claims Discipline

Do not claim:

- Revenue or productivity impact without evidence.
- Compliance or security posture unless documented.
- General availability when the docs say beta, preview, gradual rollout, or limited access.
- Integrations, platforms, or plan availability not stated in the source material.
