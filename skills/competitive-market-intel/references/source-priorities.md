# Source Priorities

Use this order when weighing evidence.

## Tier 1: Primary Sources

- Competitor changelogs, release notes, docs, pricing pages, status pages, security pages, trust centers, app marketplace listings, public webinars, press releases, official blogs, and product pages.
- Customer-facing artifacts from the company itself usually deserve the highest confidence.

## Tier 2: Strong Secondary Sources

- Analyst notes, credible trade publications, reputable podcasts or interviews, review sites with dated reviews, partner announcements, conference agendas, and public customer case studies.
- Treat these as useful but verify against primary sources when possible.

## Tier 3: Weak Or Ambiguous Signals

- Job postings, social posts, forum comments, search snippets, anonymous reviews, paid content, and unverified screenshots.
- Use these for watchlist items unless there is corroboration.

## Confidence Labels

- **High**: Direct primary source, dated, specific, and clearly related to the signal.
- **Medium**: Credible secondary source or multiple weak sources pointing to the same event.
- **Low**: Plausible but indirect signal, unclear date, or limited corroboration.

## Source Hygiene

- Prefer dated links.
- Preserve original URLs.
- Do not infer product strategy from a single vague marketing line.
- Call out when a source is old, ambiguous, sponsored, or not independently verified.
